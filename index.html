Unreel

Let friends hold you accountable.

Cut the scroll. Together.

Pitch

Gen Z spends over 3 hours a day on social media, and most existing screen-time tools are ignored. UnReel turns reduction into a shared, competitive experience. Users set personal goals, create custom challenges, and track progress with friends in real time.

The key idea is that willpower is not the main issue—social isolation is. UnReel adds accountability where people currently have none.

Why this problem matters

Young adults average about 3 hours of social media daily, and this life stage is when habits are formed that can persist for decades. Excessive use in this window is tied to poorer self-image, weaker real-world social skills, and reduced attention span.

During needfinding, we found people already know they scroll too much. Awareness is not the gap. Motivation and accountability are.

Why our solution is novel

Existing tools frame overuse as an individual discipline problem. UnReel uses friend connections to make progress social, visible, and harder to ignore.

Instead of forcing lockouts, UnReel emphasizes shared reflection and friendly competition so users reduce use by choice.

Closest competitor

The closest product is OneSec, which introduces delays before opening distracting apps. This can interrupt impulses, but delays may become normalized and features can be disabled. UnReel focuses on sustained motivation through comparison and accountability with friends.

Product Walkthrough

Home shows personal stats (reels watched, time spent, and goal progress). Leaderboard introduces social pressure with real-time ranking against friends. Goals supports app-specific limits on your own terms. Challenges turns those goals into group competitions.

Early testers were confused by Goals vs Challenges, so we redesigned both flows with clearer labels and separate entry points. We also simplified layout hierarchy so key actions are obvious without explanation.
